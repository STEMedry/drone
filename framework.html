Cognitive Development and Disciplinary Integration

The curriculum fosters a progressive escalation of disciplinary integration, transitioning from (multidisciplinary) to (interdisciplinary) and ultimately (transdisciplinary) learning, in alignment with Bloom’s Taxonomy of Cognitive Domains. It guides students from lower-order thinking (remember, understand & apply) to higher-order thinking (analyze, evaluate, and create).

In the (multidisciplinary) phase, students use knowledge of science, technology, and mathematics learned in ordinary lessons to solve foundational problems.

In the (interdisciplinary) phase, they integrate and test their knowledge in interdisciplinary scenarios.

In the (transdisciplinary) phase, students engage in engineering design tasks (automatic drone-based water sampling system), synthesizing their learning to solve real-world challenges while demonstrating critical thinking, creativity, and computational thinking.
