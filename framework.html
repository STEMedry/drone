Disciplinary Integration
and Cognitive Development ▶

The curriculum fosters a progressive escalation of disciplinary integration in alignment with Bloom’s Taxonomy of Cognitive Domains. It guides students from lower-order thinking (remember, understand & apply) to higher-order thinking (analyze, evaluate, and create).

In the initial phase (multidisciplinary), students use knowledge of science, technology, and mathematics learned in ordinary lessons to solve foundational problems.

In the intermediate phase (interdisciplinary), they integrate and test their knowledge in interdisciplinary scenarios.

In the final phase (transdisciplinary), students engage in engineering design tasks (automatic drone-based water sampling system), synthesizing their learning to solve real-world challenges while demonstrating critical thinking, creativity, and computational thinking.

Lesson Topic ▶
  • L01 (ST): Drone Technology & Piloting Skills
  • L02 (ST): Newton’s Laws of Motion & Flight Principle (1)
  • L03 (MS): Flight Principle (2) & Horizontal Flight Speed
  • L04 (MS): Angle of View (AOV) of Drone Camera
  • L05 (TM): Drone Coding & Circumnavigation
  • L06 (TM): Path Programming Design
  • L07 (STEM): Drone-assisted Water Sampling System Design
  • L08 (STEM): Automatic Drone Water Sampling and Delivery
